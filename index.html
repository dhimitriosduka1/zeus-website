Homogeneous participating medium.

To visually capture the essence of the unknown within our scene, we turned to the mysterious and captivating effects of fog. We implemented a homogeneous participating medium into our renderer that allowed us to replicate the mysterious nature of fog, effectively setting the stage for our exploration of the unknown.

Shading normals.

In our pursuit of achieving complex details in our final image, we opted to use shading normals, commonly referred to as normal maps, to enhance the realism of surfaces. This method proved to be more efficient than exporting a complex mesh, as it allowed us to simulate more intricate details without the need for increased geometric complexity.

Image Denoising.

To ensure a pristine final image, we leveraged the capabilities of the Intel Open Image Denoise library. We decided to implement it in the form of a post-process. The implementation was pretty straightforward. We had to download the prebuilt binaries and use the simple yet flexible C/C++ API.
