Features

In this section, we delve into the various features that were crucial in crafting our final image. Each chosen feature played an important role in bringing the theme "Journey to the Unknown" to life, meticulously shaping every visual aspect to resonate with the essence of exploration and mystery.

Heterogeneous participating medium.

To infuse our scene with a sense of enigmatic depth and to further enhance the theme of a 'Journey to the Unknown,' we aspired to incorporate a bed of clouds. These clouds were not just to serve as a visual element but also to act as a metaphorical blanket, shrouding the depths that lie beneath. To realize this vision, we implemented a heterogeneous participating medium within our renderer. This sophisticated feature allowed us to simulate the intricate light scattering and absorption effects of real clouds, providing a more dynamic and textured appearance. The resulting cloud bed added a layer of complexity and realism to the scene, amplifying the sense of vastness and the unknown as it interacts with the other elements and the light from the environment, enhancing the overall ethereal and mysterious atmosphere of our final image.

Homogeneous participating medium.

To visually capture the essence of the unknown within our scene, we turned to the mysterious and captivating effects of fog. We implemented a homogeneous participating medium into our renderer that allowed us to replicate the mysterious nature of it, effectively setting the stage for our exploration of the unknown.

Environment Mapping.

To fully implement our scene, we needed it to be placed in an environment that complemented the narrative. For the mystical atmosphere that we aimed to capture, our scene was designed to unfold under a night sky around Jupiter. To achieve this, we used environment mapping, a technique that allowed us to project a panoramic night sky onto the background of our image.

Shading normals.

In our pursuit of achieving complex details in our final image, we opted to use shading normals, commonly referred to as normal maps, to enhance the realism of surfaces. This method proved to be more efficient than exporting a complex mesh, as it allowed us to simulate more intricate details without the need for increased geometric complexity.

Image Denoising.

To ensure a pristine final image, we leveraged the capabilities of the Intel Open Image Denoise library. We opted for its integration as a post-processing step. This implementation process was relatively straightforward. It required us to download the pre-built binaries and use the library's simple, yet versatile, C/C++ API.
